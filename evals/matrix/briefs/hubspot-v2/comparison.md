# Comparison — one platform vs. the patchwork stack (HubSpot)

Goal: win the evaluation against the default alternative — a stack of stitched
point solutions. Audience: an evaluator with a shortlist and a spreadsheet.
Funnel: decision. Comparison grammar: the claim, the honest differences, the
receipts (switch stories, numbers), then the next step. Overselling reads as
insecurity — keep the register calm.

Voice: warm, editorial, confident, sentence case; headings may close with a
period. The comparison target is the PATTERN (a patchwork of tools), never a
named competitor.

## Must-hold facts
- The claim: one customer platform vs. five disconnected tools — the cost is in
  the seams (handoffs, syncs, licenses, reporting drift).
- Proof: **299,000+ customers in 135+ countries**; switch story from a team that
  consolidated five tools.

## Copy block
- **Eyebrow:** Platform vs. patchwork
- **Headline:** The most expensive part of your stack is the seams.
- **Subhead:** Every sync, handoff, and re-key between point tools taxes the
  team. One platform removes the tax.
- **The differences (four parallel comparison cells):**
  1. **One record vs. five copies** — marketing, sales, and service read the
     same customer, so context survives every handoff.
  2. **One admin vs. five renewals** — one permission model, one bill, one
     place to govern data.
  3. **Agents with full context vs. bots in silos** — Breeze agents work across
     the whole funnel because the data already lives together.
  4. **One report vs. a reconciliation project** — funnel numbers agree by
     construction, not by Friday-night spreadsheet.
- **Switcher quote (the proof register for this page):** "We replaced five tools
  in one quarter. The surprise wasn't the license savings — it was how much
  faster handoffs got." — Operations lead, mid-market team
- **Outcome stat band (one beat, calm):**
  - `5→1` tools consolidated in the switch story
  - `1` source of funnel truth
  - `135+` countries on the platform
- **Closing beat (the next step):** heading "Run the comparison on your own
  funnel.", support line — see your use case on the platform in thirty minutes —
  actions **Get a demo** (primary) · **Get started free** (quiet).

## Deliberate omissions
- No named competitors, no feature-checkbox war — the pattern is the rival.
- No early capture form; evaluators get the comparison content freely, the
  close offers the conversation.
- No pricing tiers — the pricing page owns that; link register only if needed.
