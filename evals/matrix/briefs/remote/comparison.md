# Comparison — your own entity vs. an employer of record (Remote)

Goal: win the build-vs-buy evaluation for international hiring. Audience: a
finance or people lead deciding whether to open a local entity or hire through
an EOR. Funnel: decision. Comparison grammar: the honest claim, the real
trade-offs, the receipts, the next step. This page respects that an entity IS
sometimes the right answer — honesty is the persuasion.

Voice: plainspoken, systematic, operational; UPPERCASE eyebrows; sentence-case
headings; no exclamation marks. Numbers carry the argument.

## Must-hold facts
- The comparison: **opening your own entity vs. hiring through Remote's EOR** —
  time, cost, liability, and exit paths.
- Entity setup: **3–9 months and five-figure setup costs** in most countries,
  plus ongoing filings; an EOR hire starts in **days**.
- Remote owns its employment infrastructure in **170+ countries** — no
  third-party chains.
- The honest line: at roughly **15–25 people in one country**, an entity often
  wins — and Remote helps teams transition when they get there.

## Copy block
- **Eyebrow:** `ENTITY VS EOR`
- **Headline:** Hire in days, or incorporate in months
- **Subhead:** Both are legitimate. The right answer depends on headcount,
  horizon, and how much liability you want to own.
- **The differences (four parallel comparison cells):**
  1. **Time to first hire** — entity: 3–9 months of registration, tax accounts,
     and payroll setup. EOR: days — the infrastructure already exists.
  2. **Cost shape** — entity: five-figure setup plus fixed annual filings,
     whatever your headcount. EOR: a flat per-employee fee that scales down as
     easily as up.
  3. **Who carries the liability** — entity: you, in a legal system you're new
     to. EOR: Remote is the employer of record; compliance is our job.
  4. **The exit** — entity: winding down takes as long as setting up. EOR:
     offboard compliantly and stop paying.
- **Switcher quote:** "We modeled the entity twice and chose the EOR both
  times. Nine months of setup for two hires never penciled out." — CFO,
  60-person company
- **Break-even beat (the honest stat band):**
  - `days` to hire through the EOR
  - `3–9 months` to open a typical entity
  - `~15–25` people in-country — where an entity starts to win
- **Closing beat:** heading "Run your own numbers", support line — bring your
  countries and headcount; we'll model both paths with you — actions
  **Talk to our team** (primary) · **See EOR pricing** (quiet).
