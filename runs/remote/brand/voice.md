# Remote — voice & creative direction

Grounded in the captured homepage copy (saved DOM) and the full-page screenshot.

## Voice

- **Plainspoken infrastructure confidence.** Headlines are declarative product
  claims in sentence case: "Global employment infrastructure", "Hire and manage
  talent in 170+ countries — fast, compliant, and secure." No exclamation marks,
  no hype adjectives; scale numbers do the persuading (170+ countries, 4,000+
  reviews, decades of local expertise).
- **Second person, operational verbs.** "Hire, manage, and pay your global team",
  "Consolidate your multi-country payroll", "Offer localized benefits". The
  reader is an operator being handed controls, not a dreamer being sold a vision.
- **Question-form conversion beats.** Mid-page banners ask a direct question
  ("Ready to see how Remote can help your team go global?") answered by exactly
  one pill: "Talk to our team" / "Book a demo".
- **Proof is outsourced to third parties.** Customer logos, named testimonials
  with real titles, G2/Trustpilot/Capterra chips, award badges — the copy itself
  stays modest and lets the badge wall shout.
- **Technical honesty at depth.** Lower sections speak API-first: "MCP agents",
  "developer-grade API", "owned entities, not partners" — jargon is allowed once
  the reader has scrolled into evaluation mode.

## Creative direction (screenshot inventory)

- Hero: pastel noise-art panel (grey-green-blue, ~#dae2e8 average), dark ink
  headline ~46px, globe illustration with orbiting product-UI chips (currency
  amounts, avatars, country flags) on the right; two pills (blue filled + blue
  outlined) under the copy.
- Rhythm: hero panel → logo marquee → maroon-accented accordion story → blue
  infra panel → gray banner beat → white workflow cards on navy media → partner
  row → testimonial cards → badge wall → closing noise-art CTA → light footer.
  One continuous light canvas end-to-end; sections separated by breathing room
  (48–112px), never by surface cuts.
- Emphasis devices: ONE inverted deep-maroon card in a list of quiet rows;
  deep-navy media windows inside white cards; color-coded uppercase eyebrows.
- Iconography: thin-line glyphs in ink or crimson; pale-blue (#ccdfff) circular
  chips for list bullets on light; periwinkle (#9bc1ff) chips on navy.
- Overall temperature: cool, systematic, optimistic — closer to a fintech
  dashboard than a travel brochure. Density medium, variance low, motion medium
  (marquee + hover choreography at 500ms).

## Content grammar for generation

- Headings: sentence case, concrete noun phrases; the biggest number in the
  section belongs in the heading or the eyebrow, not buried in body copy.
- Eyebrows: 2–5 words, uppercase, category labels ("HOW WE DO IT", "GLOBAL
  COMPANIES GROW WITH REMOTE").
- Body: 1–3 sentences per block, 18px, gray #595b5f; bold only for inline
  product terms.
- CTAs: verb-first, 2–4 words ("Book a demo", "Sign up", "Talk to our team",
  "Become a Partner"). One primary per section, secondary optional in the hero.
