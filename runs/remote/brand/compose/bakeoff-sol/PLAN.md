# Plan

## Concept

**From workforce records to decision clarity.** The page treats Workforce Intelligence as an operational decision layer for a distributed company: first expose the cost of fragmented reporting, then show one connected workflow from a leadership question to a review-ready answer.

## Audience and conversion

- VP People: understand workforce shape, movement, and where intervention is needed.
- CFO: review workforce plans with consistent definitions and a traceable source.
- HR Operations: replace recurring spreadsheet assembly with a repeatable review flow.
- Primary conversion: request a tailored demo. The form asks for role, company size, and operating footprint to support qualification without over-collecting.

## Narrative order

1. Launch hero — product promise and demo action.
2. Problem — three failure modes of fragmented workforce reporting.
3. Value — an accordion of four practical capabilities.
4. How it works — connect, organize, investigate, align.
5. Leadership views — focused outcomes for People, Finance, and HR Operations.
6. Comparison — the current workflow versus Workforce Intelligence.
7. Customer proof — existing, attributed Remote customer statements only; no product-specific implication.
8. Trust — operational controls and infrastructure context, without unsupported certifications or product claims.
9. FAQ — concise buying and implementation questions.
10. Lead form — qualified demo request and clear follow-up expectation.

## Design-system mapping

- Hero reuses `hero-inset-noise-panel` with the existing noise art and globe/product UI illustration.
- Capability exploration adapts `feature-accordion-deep-accent`.
- How-it-works and role views adapt `features-card-grid-navy-media`.
- Proof adapts `testimonial-card-row` with only canonical, attributed Remote quotes.
- Trust adapts `infra-proof-split` and its existing product UI asset.
- FAQ uses the renderer’s disclosure scaffold while preserving Remote’s light canvas and one-active-item state grammar.
- Lead form uses the Remote conversion scaffold and closes on the pastel art bookend.

## Copy safeguards

- No invented customer attribution, percentage, savings, adoption, accuracy, or time-to-value claim.
- Existing customer quotes are presented as proof of Remote’s broader operating platform, not as Workforce Intelligence testimonials.
- Product capability language describes workflows and user outcomes; it avoids guarantees and unverifiable technical specifics.
