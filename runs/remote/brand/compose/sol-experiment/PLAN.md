# PLAN — Global Payroll Consolidation Plan

Model: **GPT-5.6 Sol**

## Concept

Remote offers finance and people leaders a tailored **Global Payroll Consolidation Plan**: a practical assessment that maps their current country-by-country payroll setup into one operating model. The page is not a downloadable playbook or content campaign. It is a direct lead-generation page for teams already running payroll across multiple countries and feeling the cost of fragmented vendors, calendars, files, and compliance ownership.

Primary action: **Get your payroll plan**.

## Audience

- CFOs and finance leaders who need one reliable close across countries
- VP/Heads of People responsible for employee experience and local compliance
- Payroll leaders replacing a patchwork of local providers
- Operations teams evaluating whether to consolidate now or phase by country

## Narrative arc

Name the operating problem → establish trusted scale → show the core mechanism → make the workstreams concrete → quantify the infrastructure behind the offer → explain the consolidation process → support an honest operating-model decision → show ecosystem fit → clear practical objections → collect a qualified lead.

The page stays on Remote’s continuous light canvas, with one signature maroon accordion inversion and one closing noise-art band. It uses a conventional split whenever copy and media are the only two content regions. No interlocks, overlaps, floats, arbitrary gradients, or viewport-wide content scaffolds.

## Section plan and qualification

1. **`sol-hero` — `hero-inset-noise-panel`, split**
   - Content shape: complete eyebrow → display heading → body → two actions stack, counterweighted by one transparent global-payroll illustration.
   - Qualification: a conventional 50/50 hero split is exactly the captured pattern. The background is the sanctioned inset noise-art panel; content remains in the capped container.
   - Simpler choice: no poster meta rail, floating copy, or offset media. The offer is simple, so the mechanics stay simple.

2. **`sol-proof` — `logo-marquee-strip`, stack**
   - Content shape: one short centered proof caption followed by captured customer vectors.
   - Qualification: this is the exact logo-strip precondition. It uses real monochrome marks and no invented customer claims.

3. **`sol-control` — `infra-proof-split`, split**
   - Content shape: product UI on the left; a complete left-aligned eyebrow → heading → long body → action stack on the right.
   - Qualification: the copy explains infrastructure and operational visibility while the captured UI supplies proof texture. This matches the media-left split evidence.
   - Media rule: product UI occupies the captured square well using the pattern’s declared treatment; no decorative empty frame is added.

4. **`sol-workstreams` — `features-card-grid-navy-media`, 3-card grid**
   - Content shape: centered standalone header and three peer workstreams, each with one UI asset, eyebrow, heading, body, and link.
   - Qualification: three equal-rank modules fit the measured three-column card grid. Copy lengths are intentionally balanced; the pattern’s equal-height/body-slack/action-pinned contract controls the cards.
   - Spacing: captured 32px card-grid gutter, 32px card inset, and minimum body-to-action seam.

5. **`sol-scale` — generic stats flow, stack**
   - Content shape: centered standalone header plus three factual metrics: 170+ countries, one system of record, and 4,000+ reviews.
   - Qualification: repeated value/label records warrant the real `stat` renderer. No invented stats or decorative cards.

6. **`sol-process` — `feature-accordion-deep-accent`, split**
   - Content shape: a left-aligned header and four sequential implementation stages in an exclusive accordion, counterweighted by per-stage product UI.
   - Qualification: each stage has enough body copy and distinct media to justify disclosure. `exclusive: true` and one open item author the intended single-open behavior.
   - Accent discipline: this is the page’s only deep-accent interactive inversion.

7. **`sol-decision` — semantic table in generic stack**
   - Content shape: centered standalone header over stable repeated rows with three fixed columns: decision, local-vendor stack, consolidated Remote model.
   - Qualification: the material is genuinely tabular and benefits from aligned text starts. It uses the supported `table` contract rather than faking rows inside a split.
   - Container law: the table remains inside the standard capped container; no info-band/full-viewport scaffold.

8. **`sol-ecosystem` — `partner-proof-row`, stack**
   - Content shape: centered eyebrow → heading → body, a short row of captured partner logos, and one outlined action.
   - Qualification: the message is ecosystem compatibility, not customer popularity. The static colored partner row is the exact pattern precondition.

9. **`sol-faq` — FAQ stack**
   - Content shape: centered standalone header over five practical implementation questions.
   - Qualification: objections have substantive answers, so disclosure reduces scanning cost. The FAQ is exclusive with one item open; it does not borrow the signature maroon active surface already used by the process section.

10. **`sol-plan-form` — `cta-closing-noise`, stack with form**
    - Content shape: centered closing heading/body and a compact qualification form.
    - Qualification: this is the single conversion destination, so a real form belongs here. The full-bleed noise background is captured; the content stack remains at the pattern’s measured 870px width.

## Failure modes deliberately avoided

- No editorial interlock or offset device for simple content.
- No comparison split: the prior run’s comparison exceeded the container, collapsed its gutter, and lost relational spacing. This run uses a contained semantic table.
- No unsupported stat/table anatomy: both use their current explicit contracts.
- No carousel, avoiding unnecessary edge-cut behavior and its interaction burden.
- No non-exclusive accordion. Both disclosures author `exclusive: true`.
- No handwritten HTML or CSS repair. Composition is the only authoring input.
- No viewport-wide content; only sanctioned backgrounds may bleed.
- No single uniform stack gap; each selected pattern owns its captured relational spacing.
